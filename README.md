# Webapper

### Turn any website into a real desktop app — in seconds.

Gmail, Notion, ChatGPT, Spotify, WhatsApp — stop juggling a million browser tabs. Webapper wraps any website into its own dedicated window, with its own icon in your Dock or taskbar, its own login session, and none of the browser clutter.

**Free. Open source. Works on Mac, Windows, and Linux.**

## What does it do?

Your browser is great for browsing. It's not so great when you have 30 tabs open and can't find the one with your emails.

Webapper lets you take any website — your work tools, your favourite apps, anything — and give it its own window, its own place in your Dock or taskbar, and its own separate login. It looks and feels like a real app, because it is one.

---

## Features

**Stop living in browser tabs**
Each app gets its own window. Alt-tab straight to Gmail. Cmd-tab straight to Notion. No more hunting through tabs.

**Separate logins for everything**
Each app is completely isolated. Log into your work Google account in Gmail and your personal one in Google Drive — at the same time, no conflicts.

**Incognito mode**
Need an app that never saves cookies or login sessions? One click and it's gone when you close the window.

**Chrome extension support**
Install uBlock Origin, SponsorBlock, or any Chrome extension and it applies across all your web apps automatically.

**Built-in ad blocking**
Ads and trackers are blocked by default across every app you create. No setup needed.

**Make it yours**
Give each app a custom name, upload your own icon, or let Webapper fetch the favicon automatically.

**20 popular apps ready to go**
Gmail, Notion, Slack, Figma, ChatGPT, Claude, GitHub, Spotify, WhatsApp, YouTube and more — add any of them in one click from the built-in gallery.

**Remembers everything**
Window sizes, positions, logins — everything is saved. Your apps pick up exactly where you left off.

## Getting Started

### 1. Install Webapper

Download the installer for your platform above and open it. On macOS, drag Webapper to your Applications folder.

### 2. Create your first app

Click **"New App"** in the bottom left, paste in a URL (like `https://mail.google.com`), give it a name, and hit **Create App**.

### 3. Launch it

Double-click the app card. It opens in its own window — just like a native app.

That's it.

## Installing Extensions

Go to the **Extensions** tab, paste in a Chrome Web Store link, and click Install. That's all — the extension works across every app you create.

Popular ones to try:

- **[AdBlocker Ulimate](https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/adblocker-ultimate/ohahllgiabjaoigichmmfljhkcfikeof)** — extra ad blocking on top of the built-in blocker
- **[SponsorBlock](https://chrome.google.com/webstore/detail/sponsorblock-for-youtube/mnjggcdmjocbbbhaepdhchncahnbgone)** — skip sponsored segments on YouTube

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is it free?**
Yes, completely free and open source forever.

**Does it work with my existing logins?**
Yes. When you sign into an app inside Webapper, it saves your session just like a browser would. You won't need to log in again.

**Will my apps slow down my computer?**
Each app is a lightweight window. Having a few open is comparable to having a few browser tabs open.

**Can I use it with Google, Microsoft, or Apple sign-in?**
Yes. Sign-in popups open automatically and close themselves once you're logged in.

**Does it block ads?**
Yes. Ad and tracker blocking is built in and on by default.

**What if a website doesn't work properly?**
Most websites work out of the box. If something looks broken, try creating the app in **Incognito** mode.
